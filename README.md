# Padiyathalawa-dolerite-dyke-analysis
Field-based structural interpretation of a dolerite dyke from Padiyathalawa, Sri Lanka
# 🌍 Structural Analysis of Dolerite Dykes, Padiyathalawa (Sri Lanka)

---

## 📌 Project Overview
Dolerite occurs as intrusive dykes cutting across metamorphic country rocks in the Padiyathalawa area. This project presents a field-based structural interpretation of the dolerite dyke, focusing on joint systems, fracture patterns, and structural orientation using field observations, stereonet analysis, and rosette diagram interpretation.

---

## 🪨 Geological Setting
The dolerite bodies are dark-colored, fine- to medium-grained mafic intrusions that are relatively resistant to weathering. They cut across metamorphic rocks of the Vijayan Complex and represent post-tectonic mafic magmatic events.

According to Arachchi et al. (2017), doleritic intrusions within the Vijayan Complex were emplaced after regional metamorphism and are related to younger mafic magmatic activity.

---

## 🧪 Lithology and Composition
The dolerite is a dark-colored, fine- to medium-grained mafic intrusive rock. Based on field observations, it is interpreted to be mainly composed of plagioclase and pyroxene minerals typical of doleritic composition.

The exact mineralogical composition is not confirmed in this study as no thin section or laboratory analysis was conducted.

---

## 🔬 Structural Features

### 🧭 Joint Systems
- Well-developed joint systems are observed in the dolerite dyke  
- Joint planes are systematic and more regular compared to surrounding metamorphic rocks  
- Dominant joint orientations trend NW–SE (based on rosette diagram analysis)  
- Most joints are steeply dipping, indicating brittle deformation  

---

### 📊 Stereonet Analysis
- Stereonet data shows clustering of joint orientations  
- Dominant NW-trending joint sets are observed  
- Indicates structurally controlled fracture development  
- Suggests influence of cooling contraction and regional stress fields  

---

## 📊 Methods
- Field observation of dolerite dyke exposure  
- Identification of joint planes in outcrop  
- Interpretation of joint orientations from field data  
- Stereonet plotting of structural data  
- Rosette diagram analysis to determine dominant joint trends  

---

## 🧠 Interpretation
The joint systems in the dolerite are interpreted to have formed mainly due to cooling contraction after magma emplacement, with additional influence from regional tectonic stresses.

The difference in joint orientation between dolerite and surrounding metamorphic rocks suggests that the intrusion experienced a distinct post-emplacement stress regime.

Because dolerite is relatively homogeneous, it developed more regular joint spacing and orientation compared to heterogeneous metamorphic host rocks.

---

## 🌊 Weathering and Hydrogeology
- Spheroidal weathering is observed in dolerite bodies  
- Weathering occurs along joint planes due to chemical alteration  
- Results in rounded corestones and spheroidal weathering features  
- Joints enhance secondary permeability and groundwater movement  

---

## 🧭 Significance
This study highlights:
- Intrusive igneous processes in the Vijayan Complex  
- Structural control of joint systems in dolerite  
- Relationship between cooling, tectonics, and fracture formation  
- Influence of joints on weathering and groundwater flow
